Arctic Sea Ice Forecasting based on Multi-scale Graph Modeling with Conservation Laws

Authors: Lan Wei and Nikolaos M. Freris

Introduction

This is the demo for IceGAT, a model for predicting the sea ice concentration (SIC). Using the daily updated data from the ERA5 dataset, we plotted the ground-truth SIC for four days, followed by the prediction for one week. SIC is the sea ice-covered area relative to the total at a given location in the ocean and thus ranges from 0 (blue) to 1 (white), which corresponds to ice-free to fully ice-covered conditions. Sea ice extent (SIE) is the integral sum of the areas of all cells with at least 15% ice concentration, while sea ice area (SIA) is the integral sum of the product of ice concentration and area of all cells with at least 15% ice concentration.
